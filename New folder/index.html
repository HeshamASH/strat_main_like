A Worldwide Web of Intentions, Stakeholders, and Results

The Strategic Plan Markup Language (StratML) is an XML vocabulary and schema for strategic plans, facilitating transparency, collaboration, and accountability.

Discover the Purpose

Definition & Purposes

StratML is designed to make strategic plans more accessible, interoperable, and actionable.

  • Facilitate the sharing, referencing, discovery, and reuse of strategic plan elements.
  • Enable literal linkages for "strategic alignment" between goals, objectives, and business processes.
  • Help discover potential performance partners with common or complementary objectives.
  • Facilitate stakeholder engagement, input, and feedback on strategic goals.
  • Make strategic planning more agile and responsive, preventing plans from becoming outdated "shelfware".
  • Reduce redundant data entry and inconsistencies by referencing authoritative sources.

Supporting Open Government Mandates

StratML provides a machine-readable format to help U.S. federal agencies comply with key legislation.

GPRA Modernization Act of 2010 (GPRAMA)

Section 10 requires agencies to publish their strategic and performance plans and reports in a searchable, machine-readable format. StratML is such a format.

OPEN Government Data Act (OGDA)

Extends the requirement to all Federal records to be open, machine-readable, and compliant with standardized schemas by default.

The StratML Standard in Three Parts

The standard is broken down into three parts, covering strategic plans, performance reports, and additional elements for deeper integration.

Part 1: Strategic Plans (ISO 17469-1)

Focuses on the core vocabulary for strategic plans: Vision, Mission, Values, Goals, and Objectives.

ISO Schema Schema Documentation

Part 2: Performance Plans & Reports

Extends Part 1 to include performance indicators and stakeholder roles, enabling the documentation of value chains and tracking of results.

ANSI/AIIM 22:2017 Schema Documentation

Part 3: Additional Elements

Specifies additional elements to address the data requirements implicit in GPRAMA, focusing on deeper integration with performance data.

Prototype Services & Tools

A variety of tools and services have been developed by the community to support the creation, editing, and analysis of StratML documents.

  1. StratNavApp - Chris Fox enabled his StratNavApp to produce valid StratML output. Public plans are available here.
  2. Turn-Key Systems - StratML files can be rendered in PDF and other formats using TopLeaf XML Publisher.
  3. Vionta - Jorge Sanchez of Vionta began to develop a StratML portal running on BaseX. It is documented on GitHub.
  4. Ximdex - Colleagues at Ximdex imported many files into their StratML query service prototype.
  5. Ictect - Pradeep Jain of Ictect expressed interest in updating his StratML plug-in for MS Word. A Quick Start Guide remains available.
  6. 01 Communications - Andre Cusson of 01Communications has expressed intent to restore and update his StratML portal. See his related efforts.
  7. Joe Carmel - Joe Carmel's StratML Catalog used AJAX to index files. The most recent update is available on the StratML.us site.
  8. StratML.services - Jason Lind planned to make StratML.services an extensive platform, though the effort has apparently been abandoned.
  9. RUSTPrivacy - Gannon Dick of RUSTPrivacy.org converted the Federal Advisory Committee Act (FACA) database to StratML format. See the home page for his related efforts.
  10. oXygen - Support for the StratML standard was provided in oXygen XML Editor version 14.0. The framework is available as an open source project on GitHub.
